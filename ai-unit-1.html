Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Definition: Artificial Intelligence is the science and engineering of making intelligent machines, especially intelligent computer programs. It is related to the similar task of using computers to understand human intelligence.

Goal of AI: To create systems that exhibit intelligent behavior, learn, demonstrate, explain, and provide user advice.

Branches of AI

  • Machine Learning: Systems that learn from data
  • Natural Language Processing: Understanding human language
  • Robotics: Intelligent machines that interact with the environment
  • Computer Vision: Interpreting and understanding visual information
  • Expert Systems: Emulating decision-making ability of human experts

Applications of AI

  • Speech recognition
  • Image recognition
  • Medical diagnosis
  • Stock trading
  • Self-driving cars
  • Game playing

History of AI

Key milestones include the development of logic-based AI in the 1950s, rule-based expert systems in the 1980s, and machine learning breakthroughs in the 2000s and beyond.

Types of AI

  • Narrow AI: Performs a narrow task (e.g., facial recognition or internet searches)
  • General AI: Performs any intellectual task that a human can
  • Super AI: Surpasses human intelligence (still theoretical)

Challenges in AI

  • Data privacy
  • Bias in AI algorithms
  • Lack of explainability
  • Job displacement
